Old Talks II

Title: Algebraic number theory and the ideal class group.
Speaker: Will Youmans.
Date: Tuesday, November $26^{th}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: Over three and a half centuries ago Fermat posed his famous Last Theorem. Attempts to prove the theorem stimulated the development of algebraic number theory throughout the $19^{th}$ century. We will give an introduction to concepts in algebraic number theory from a historical perspective with the aim of motivating the ideal class group.\

Title: Euler’s $\zeta(2)$ and $\zeta(4)$ and other related series by means of trigonometric integrals.
Speaker: Cezar Lupu.
Date: Tuesday, November $21^{st}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: In this talk, we shall illustrate how we can make use of trigonometric integrals such as $$\int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin^n(x) dx, \quad \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \tan^n(x) dx$$ for derivation of Euler’s $\zeta(2)$ and $\zeta(4)$ formulas. On the other hand, we also obtain Leibniz’s alternating series for $\log 2$ and $\frac{\pi}{4}$.

Title: Geometry, Links, and Magnetism.
Speaker: Nathan Hayford.
Date: Thursday, November $14^{th}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: The linking number is a well-known invariant in topology, and has been around since the time of Gauss. We will discuss this invariant, and how it may be computed via purely geometric means. Furthermore, we investigate Gauss’ original motivations and intuition for the linking number in the theory of magnetism. Finally, if time permits, we mention how other topological invariants (like the Jones polynomial) can be computed (in a surprisingly similar manner) via appropriate topological field theories.

Title: Rogue wave solutions to the nonlocal nonlinear Schrodinger equation.
Speaker: Rusuo Ye.
Date: Thursday, October $17^{th}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: By using a matrix version of Darboux transformation with a spectral matrix of the Jordan block form, rogue wave solutions to the nonlocal nonlinear Schrödinger (NLS) equation are presented. General rogue wave solutions up to the fourth-order are formulated. The structure of these solutions is essentially determined by the corresponding solutions of the Lyapunov equation. With appropriate choices of free parameters, dynamics and distribution patterns for the nonlocal NLS equation are shown to be much abundant than those for the local NLS equation.\
