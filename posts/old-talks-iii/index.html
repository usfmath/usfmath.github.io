Title: An Introduction to the Riemann-Hilbert Problems on the real line.
Speaker: Fudong Wang.
Date: Thursday, September $26^{th}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: In this talk, we will briefly discuss what is the Riemann-Hilbert Problem(RHP). We will focus on the situation where the contour is real line. Then formulate the RHP in $L^2$-space (hopefully). The Sokhotski-Plemelj formula (or Cauchy operator) plays a fundamental role in the theory of RHP, from where we can see the connection between the singular integral equation and the RHP. A solution method, via the Cauchy operator, will also be introduced (hopefully). Finally, we will discuss how does RHP connect to the MKdV equation.\

Title: Rational Solutions to the Painlevé II equation.
Speaker: Nathan Hayford.
Date: Thursday, September $19^{th}$
Time: 2:00pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: The Painlevé II (PII) equation appears in many problems in theoretical physics, combinatorics, and random matrix theory. However, being a non-linear equation, solutions can be difficult to construct. One may wonder if there is a way to construct new solutions from old ones, and in fact there is. We discuss the Bäcklund transformation structure of PII with an explicit example: a family of rational solutions to this equation.\
