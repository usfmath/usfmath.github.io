Old Talks IV

Title: Modeling Physical Phenomenon: A derivation and exploration of Laplacian growth.
Speaker: Nathan Hayford.
Date: Thursday, August $29^{th}$
Time: 3:30pm
Place: CMC 109
Abstract: Often the most interesting mathematical problems are physically motivated: Laplacian growth is no exception. However, one may wonder how such models are derived, i.e., what is their physical relevance, what constitutes a good balance between an easily solvable problem and a physically realistic one, among other questions. In this talk, I plan on deriving Laplacian growth from first principles physics, and explain the original motivations of the equation (in short, modeling a 2D fluid domain’s expansion/retraction as fluid is pumped in/out from above). I will briefly discuss the integrable structure of Laplacian growth, and how it leads to exact solutions of the equation; finally, if time permits, I will mention another modern application of Laplacian growth in condensed matter theory: the integer quantum Hall effect.\

Old Talks II

Title: Algebraic number theory and the ideal class group. Speaker: Will Youmans. Date: Tuesday, November $26^{th}$ Time: 2:00pm Place: CMC 109 Abstract: Over three and a half centuries ago Fermat posed his famous Last Theorem. Attempts to prove the theorem stimulated the development of algebraic number theory throughout the $19^{th}$ century. We will give an introduction to concepts in algebraic number theory from a historical perspective with the aim of motivating the ideal class group.

Old Talks I

Title: The Calderón problem.
Speaker: Lina Fajardo Gomez.
Date: Thursday, February $13^{th}$
Time: 11:00am
Place: CIS 1023
Abstract: What do oil drilling and tumor detection have in common? How could nonlinear equations be easier to work with? The Calderón problem was first introduced as an inverse boundary value problem: how information about the boundary of a domain can be used to determine information about its interior. We will discuss the set up, some applications, and a proposed solution for a class of Calderón type inverse problems.
